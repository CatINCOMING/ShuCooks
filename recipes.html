Jambalaya (California Pizza Kitchen Style)

A spicy, rich, pasta-style jambalaya inspired by the classic California Pizza Kitchen version.

Ingredients (Serves 4)

  • 3 tbsp Cajun spice
  • 12–16 deveined shrimp
  • ½ red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, diced
  • ½ large red onion, thinly diced
  • ½ pound cooked linguine pasta
  • 1 andouille sausage, sliced
  • 1 ½ cups chicken stock
  • 1 tbsp tomato sauce
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Season the vegetables:
    In a large bowl, combine the bell pepper, jalapeño, red onion, garlic, Cajun seasoning, and black pepper. Toss thoroughly until evenly coated.
  2. Sear the shrimp:
    Heat 1–2 tablespoons of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp and sear for 30 seconds to 1 minute per side, just until opaque. Remove and set aside.
  3. Brown the sausage:
    In the same skillet, add extra oil if needed. Add the sliced andouille sausage and sauté for about 1 minute.
  4. Cook the vegetables:
    Add the seasoned vegetable mixture. Sear for 3–4 minutes until softened and edges begin to curl.
  5. Build the sauce:
    Add the chicken stock and tomato sauce. Stir to combine, scraping up browned bits from the pan. Simmer and reduce for 5 minutes.
  6. Incorporate the pasta:
    Add the cooked linguine and toss for 2–3 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats the pasta evenly.
